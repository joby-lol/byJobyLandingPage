Where I work

Aside from my many, many hobbies and occasional freelance work (when I have time for it in between hobbies, anyway), I'm in my fifth year as a graphic designer at the New Mexico Lottery. I do a lot of things there, from print design, to videography, to sign production and installation, to my specialty: web development.

What I do

My hobbies include computer tinkering, privacy worrying, obstacle course running, science fiction, accumulating unfinished undergraduate degrees, and obsessively gathering data about myself. Basically I love science, I love computers, I love technology, and I can't even help myself when I try to make the present more like the future I imagine.

I've been building websites for fun and profit since I was in middle school. Doug Fort once mailed his nephew, a friend of mine, a book about HTML 4. I promptly read it cover to cover and never looked back.

Now I'm lucky enough to do what I've done for fun since middle school for a living, and I like that very much.

Where to find me

My web presence waxes and wanes with the fickle nature of my obsessions. At the moment this page is the extent of my website (pretty though, ain't it?).
